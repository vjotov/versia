Setting up a TeamWare Environment

Related Topics:

Basic Concepts of Sun WorkShop TeamWare Administrative Issues

In the beginning of a project, before team development starts, the following happens:

1. One team member sets up your team's parent workspaces to house separate phases and divisions of your project, such as:

  • Development--the workspace where your team's latest files reside
  • Integration--the workspace where team leads combine everyone's work
  • Nightly builds--the workspace where your buildmaster creates whole executable applications from your files
  • Different versions of builds based on criteria such as project phase, release, platform, or locale

2. Before starting to work on files, each team member sets up his or her own child workspace in relation to the parent workspace.

3. Each team member brings over from the parent workspace a copy of the directories and files he or she will need to modify.

Now the team's environment is set up for concurrent development.
